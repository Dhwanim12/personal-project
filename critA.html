Criterion A

Report Runthrough of Criterion A



Strand i

State a learning goal for your project. The learning goal is what you want to learn through the process of completing the project. This learning goal should also relate to your personal interest. It should be something that you are curious and passionate about, as this will reflect on your product and report and affect the time and effort you put into the project. Additionally, the learning goal should be challenging, extensive, and multifacetedas this will allow you to conduct detailed research and provide you with a challenge to solve over the next 6-9 months.

How to choose a learning goal guide

Strand ii

State a product goal for your project. The product goal is what you want to create at the end of the project. Your product goal also needs to be multidimensional, meaning it can be judged based on many different aspects. It should also meet a need in the community, or assist your local, school or global community in some way. Then you need to create a success critieria for the product, where you need to give different levels of achievement for different dimensions, such as aesthetics, costs, customer, function, materials, and size. The success criteria should follow the SMART framework, and you will use it in Criterion C to judge your product and give it a rating out of 7. Lastly, it is important to explain and justify the criteria, and why you have chosen to include certain dimensions. This should be backed up by primary and secondary research.

How to choose a product goal guide

Strand iii

Create a detailed action plan which states the expected date, the task you need to complete, the success criteria dimension it follows, the ATL skill it is linked to, whether it was completed on time or not, and if not the new date it was completed on and the rationale of this. Take into account your schoolâ€™s deadlines while creating this action plan, and ensure to follow this thoroughly to get enough time to complete the project. Consider how long will it take to research, design, develop, and launch your product. What tasks need to be completed along the way, and what resources will be required to meet these goals. By breaking down the project into smaller, more manageable steps, you can ensure that the project stays on track and that the end result is a high-quality product that meets the needs of your target audience.
