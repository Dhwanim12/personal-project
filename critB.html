Criterion B

In Criterion B of the Personal Project, students have to evaluate what ATL skill they used to acheive their learning goal and their product goal (these two need to be different skills). Once the skill is identified, students can find the MYP ATL skill clusters and present evidence accordingly. Below are the 5 ATL skills, their clusters, and different types of evidence that you can collect for each of them.

Communication

Communication is important because it helps students to express themselves, collaborate with others, and engage in meaningful dialogue.

Communication is a key aspect of Criterion B, as students are constantly communicating with teachers, peers, supervisors, and other stakeholders throughout the project's development. Students need to use their communication skills to seek feedback, provide updates, and present their ideas in a clear and compelling manner.

Evidence:

  • Giving and receiving meaningful feedback from your peers, mentors, and the global community. To gain feedback from the global community, make sure that your product is advertised on social media, as this will reach a larer target audience and showcase your product to make people internationally as well.
  • Interviewing people who have used your product ot rate it and justify their rating, and ive you constructive criticism, so that you can improve your final product.
  • Research and paraphrase information accurately, communicating it in your own words and citing it to prove credibility.

    • Social

    This includes developing positive relationships, engaging in active listening, expressing oneself clearly and respectfully, and understanding and responding appropriately to different cultural norms and perspectives.

    Overall, the social skill is essential for the successful completion of the Personal Project, as it involves engaging with and navigating the complex social dynamics of the project environment. By developing strong social skills, students can better communicate their ideas, collaborate effectively with others, and ultimately achieve their project goals.

    MYP ATL skill cluster

    Collaboration: Students work effectively and efficiently with their community to achieve their learning goal or their product goal

    Evidence:

  • Gaining feedback and improving your product
  • Showing compassion, empathy and kindness towards the people in your community by listening to their perspectives, opinions and viewpoints

    • Self-Managment

    Self-management is considered an important ATL (Approaches to Learning) skill that is essential for the Personal Project. Self-management is defined as the ability to regulate one's own behavior, emotions, and thoughts in a productive manner. It involves setting goals, planning and organizing tasks, monitoring progress, and adapting to changes in order to achieve those goals.

    MYP ATL skill cluster

    Organization: Students have good time-managment skills

    Evidence:

  • Short-term and Long-term action plan
  • Using a planning app where you can input your deadlines
  • Organized documentation and process journal


    • Affective: Students keep themselves motivated and in a healthy mental state throughout the project.

    Evidence:
  • Practicing mindfulness through meditation and affirmations
  • Practicing perservance and resilience by overcoming challenges and learning from them


    • Reflection: Students reflect on the learnings and mistakes they made during the process of completing the project.

    Evidence:
  • Journal questions answered about the experience and what they would do differently next time
  • Developing new focus stratergies that students developed


    • Research

    The ATL skill of research is highly crucial when completing the Personal Project, as students are independently locating and evaluating information, which is essential for success in the Personal Project.

    MYP ATL Skill CLuster

    Information Literacy: Students need to have the ability to identify, locate and evaluate, information to effectively solve a problem

    Evidence:
  • Process data and evaluate primary and secondary sources
  • Create inquiry questions and answer them in detail
  • Colleting and organizing information in an efficient and effective way


    • Media Literacy: Students need to have the study different forms of media and gather information from it

    Evidence:
  • Comparing and linking different forms of media to one another
  • Reseaching for different types of media and trackin important features of them in a journal
  • Analyzing which type of media would be the most effective and informative for you to use, and why

    • Thinking

    The ATL skill of thinking is when students use critical thinking to solve challenges or problems they might've faced during their Personal Project journey.It shows the conceptual and contextual learning that influenced the development of the Project.

    MYP ATL skill cluster

    Critical Thinking: Students need to identify patterns, and make relevant connections by comparing and contrasting and drawing conclusions. Students who have developed critical thinking skills are able to ask probing questions, consider alternative perspectives, and weigh evidence to make informed decisions.

    Evidence:
  • Identify gaps in knowledge and formulate key questions
  • Plan to achieve goals, including identifying targets and outlining steps
  • Make logical, reasonable judgments and create arguments to support them

    • Creative Thinking: Students need to generat new ideas or solutions to problems by brainstorming, lateral thinking, and visualizing possibilities. Students who have developed creative thinking skills are able to approach problems from multiple angles, take risks, and adapt their ideas to changing circumstances.

    Evidence:
  • Evaluate solutions to problems
  • Utilize old ideas in new ways and combine parts in new ways
  • Create original works and ideas and visualize alternative

    • Transfer: Students need to apply their learning and knowledge from one context to new situations or problems. Students should have skills such as recognizing patterns, generalizing concepts, and adapting strategies to new contexts. Students who have developed transfer skills are able to see connections between different areas of knowledge, apply what they have learned in new and unfamiliar contexts, and recognize the limitations of their own knowledge.

    Evidence
  • Make connections between learning gained in different subject areas
  • Applying skills and knwoledge in new contexts, subject areas and harder challenges
  • Demonstrate personal responsibility for lifelong learning
